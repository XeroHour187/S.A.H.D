The S.A.H.D Collective is a supportive and inclusive space where fathers who have chosen to prioritize caregiving for their children gather to share experiences, challenges, and triumphs. In this community, the focus extends beyond traditional gender roles, embracing the diverse roles fathers play in their families.

Men's mental and physical health challenges are openly discussed and addressed within this community. Stay-at-home dads often face unique pressures and societal expectations, which can take a toll on their well-being. By fostering open dialogue and providing mutual support, this community helps its members navigate issues such as stress, isolation, identity shifts, and the importance of self-care.

In addition to mental and physical health, the community acknowledges the daily challenges and victories that come with being a father. From diaper blowouts to bedtime stories, Stay-At-Home Dads celebrate the small wins and offer empathy during the tough moments. They share tips, strategies, and advice on parenting, household management, and balancing responsibilities.

As a bonus, this community also serves as a platform for Stay-At-Home Dads to bond over their love for gaming, and organizing online gaming sessions. Through virtual meetups and gaming forums, members forge meaningful connections, exchange gaming tips, and build friendships rooted in their shared passion for gaming and fatherhood.

Ultimately, The S.A.H.D Collective is a testament to the strength, resilience, and love that fathers bring to their families each day. It's a space where men can find solidarity, support, and inspiration as they navigate the rewarding yet challenging journey of fatherhood.

Where are we now?

'The S.A.H.D Collective' is currently in it's start-up phase. Member count and activity is low, however it will grow over time as we expand via outreach through various social media outlets.

What is our endgame?

The Current endgame for 'The S.A.H.D Collective' is to have an engaging community that spands the globe raising awareness and support for men, men's rights, and men's health. While offering both a virtual and in person community, and support system for those that have a need for it.
